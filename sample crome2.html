How to Bring Moroccan Decor Ideas Into Your Home

ByAshley
Save for Later!

Ever thought your living room could transport you straight to the bustling markets of Marrakech? Moroccan decor isn’t just for luxury resorts or far-flung getaways—it’s a vibrant style you can easily (and affordably!) bring into your own space.

What Makes Moroccan Decor So Magical?

Moroccan decor is a melting pot of cultures, blending African, Arab, and European influences into a rich tapestry of colors, textures, and patterns. It’s all about creating an inviting atmosphere that feels both exotic and cozy.

Key Elements of Moroccan Decor

Bold Colors and Rich Hues

Think deep reds, vibrant oranges, royal blues, and lush purples. These colors evoke the Moroccan landscape—from the Sahara sands to the Mediterranean coast.

Intricate Patterns and Textures

Geometric patterns, mosaics, and ornate carvings are hallmarks of this style. You’ll find these designs on everything from tiles to textiles.

Ambient Lighting

Moroccan lanterns made of brass or wrought iron cast mesmerizing shadows and add a warm glow to any room.

Luxurious Textiles

Plush rugs, embroidered cushions, and flowing curtains create a layered, inviting space that’s perfect for lounging.

Natural Materials

Wood, ceramics, and metals like brass and copper add an earthy touch that grounds the vibrant colors and patterns.

How to Bring Moroccan Flair into Your Home

Start with a Statement Piece

Swap out your coffee table for a carved wooden piece or a mosaic-tiled table. This instantly sets the tone for the rest of the room.

Layer Your Textiles

Get crafty! Drape colorful fabrics over your sofa or use them as wall hangings. Pile on some patterned cushions and throw blankets for extra comfort.

Light It Up

Opt for Moroccan-style lanterns or lamps. You can even DIY your own by adding intricate metalwork (or a metal-look stencil) to existing fixtures.

Incorporate Patterns

Don’t be shy with patterns. Mix and match geometric designs on rugs, cushions, and curtains. The key is to stay within your chosen color palette to keep things cohesive.

Accessorize with Purpose

Add ceramics, metal trays, and decorative mirrors. These small touches can make a big impact (without breaking the bank!).

Budget-Friendly Tips

  • Thrift Shopping Treasures: Hunt for unique pieces at thrift stores or flea markets. You might find a hidden gem that fits perfectly with the Moroccan theme.
  • DIY Decor: Create your own mosaic frames using broken tiles or glass. Paint terra cotta pots with vibrant designs—it’s fun and adds a personal touch.
  • Fabric Finds: Use scarves or fabric remnants as table runners or wall hangings. They’re inexpensive and add that splash of color and pattern.
  • Swap and Upcycle: Repurpose items you already have. An old wooden tray can become a stunning centerpiece with a bit of paint and creativity.

Main Points to Keep in Mind

  • Stick to a Color Scheme: Choose 2-3 main colors to keep your space harmonious.
  • Balance is Key: While bold is beautiful, balance vibrant elements with neutral tones.
  • Mix Textures: Combine smooth ceramics with plush fabrics and rough-hewn woods for depth.
  • Don’t Overcrowd: Moroccan decor is rich, but your space shouldn’t feel cluttered. Select pieces you love and let them shine.
  • Personalize It: Incorporate personal mementos or souvenirs that complement the style.

 

1. Ornate Tilework for Walls

Embrace the heart of Moroccan decor with stunning tilework for your walls. Moroccan tiles, known for their intricate geometric patterns and bold colors, are perfect for creating a statement in any room. Use them as a backsplash in your kitchen, a feature wall in your living room, or an accent in your bathroom. The combination of cobalt blue, deep red, emerald green, and saffron yellow tiles can instantly transport you to a Moroccan riad.

The beauty of Moroccan tiles lies in their versatility. You can mix and match different patterns to create a unique design that reflects your personality. For a more traditional look, opt for classic zellige tiles, handcrafted from clay and glazed to perfection. These tiles add a touch of authenticity and craftsmanship to your space.

To enhance the effect, complement the tilework with white or neutral-colored walls. This allows the vibrant patterns to stand out without overwhelming the room. Add some Moroccan lanterns or sconces to complete the look, casting intricate shadows that enhance the exotic atmosphere.

2. Intricate Carved Wood Furniture

Moroccan decor often features intricately carved wooden furniture that exudes charm and sophistication. Look for pieces like hand-carved coffee tables, armoires, and headboards that showcase traditional Moroccan craftsmanship. The detailed patterns and motifs carved into the wood create a sense of luxury and heritage.

When selecting Moroccan furniture, opt for rich, dark woods like walnut or mahogany, which enhance the opulent feel of the decor. These pieces often come adorned with brass or copper accents, adding a touch of metallic shine that complements the warm tones of the wood.

Pair your carved furniture with plush textiles and cushions in jewel tones. Think deep purples, blues, and reds with gold embroidery or tassels. This combination of textures and colors creates a cozy and inviting space that embodies the essence of Moroccan design.

3. Moroccan Lanterns for Ambient Lighting

One of the most iconic elements of Moroccan decor is the use of lanterns to create ambient lighting. Moroccan lanterns, often made of metal and glass, come in a variety of shapes and sizes, each casting beautiful, intricate patterns when lit. Hang them from the ceiling, place them on tables, or use them as floor lamps to bring a warm, inviting glow to your space.

For an authentic Moroccan look, choose lanterns with colored glass panels. These lanterns diffuse light in a way that creates a magical, almost mystical atmosphere. The interplay of light and shadow adds depth and intrigue to any room.

Mix and match different lantern styles and sizes to create a layered lighting effect. Whether you’re illuminating a cozy corner or making a grand statement in your living room, Moroccan lanterns are sure to enhance the exotic charm of your home.

4. Vibrant Moroccan Rugs

Moroccan rugs are a must-have for adding texture and color to your home. These rugs, often handwoven by artisans, feature bold geometric patterns and vibrant hues. Place them in your living room, bedroom, or even your kitchen to instantly warm up the space and add a touch of Moroccan flair.

When choosing a Moroccan rug, consider the type of pattern and color scheme that best fits your decor. Beni Ourain rugs, with their simple black and white diamond patterns, are perfect for a more minimalist look. For a bolder statement, opt for Azilal or Boucherouite rugs, which come in a riot of colors and intricate designs.

Layering rugs is another great way to enhance the Moroccan aesthetic. Combine different sizes and patterns to create a rich, textured look that feels cozy and inviting. These rugs not only add visual interest but also provide a soft, comfortable surface underfoot.

5. Moroccan Poufs for Versatile Seating

Moroccan poufs are not only stylish but also incredibly versatile. These leather or fabric-covered poufs can be used as extra seating, footrests, or even side tables. They come in a variety of colors and patterns, making them a perfect addition to any room.

Choose poufs in rich, jewel tones like emerald green, sapphire blue, or ruby red to add a pop of color to your space. For a more neutral look, opt for poufs in shades of brown, tan, or white. The intricate embroidery or patchwork on these poufs adds a touch of Moroccan craftsmanship and detail.

Scatter a few poufs around your living room or bedroom to create a cozy, laid-back atmosphere. They are perfect for casual seating and can easily be moved around to suit your needs. Pair them with a Moroccan rug and some plush cushions for the ultimate in comfort and style.

6. Mosaic Tables for a Touch of Artistry

Mosaic tables are a quintessential element of Moroccan decor. These tables feature intricate tile patterns set into the tabletop, creating a stunning piece of functional art. Whether used as coffee tables, side tables, or dining tables, mosaic tables add a touch of Moroccan artistry to your home.

Look for tables with bold, geometric patterns in vibrant colors. The combination of different shapes and hues creates a visually striking effect that draws the eye. These tables often have wrought iron bases, adding to their rustic, handmade appeal.

Place a mosaic table in your living room or on your patio to create a focal point. Pair it with some Moroccan lanterns and lush greenery to enhance the exotic atmosphere. The craftsmanship and detail of these tables make them a true statement piece.

7. Moroccan Mirrors for Reflective Beauty

Mirrors play a significant role in Moroccan decor, often featuring ornate frames with intricate carvings or metalwork. These mirrors not only serve a functional purpose but also add a decorative element that enhances the overall aesthetic of the room.

Choose mirrors with frames that incorporate traditional Moroccan designs, such as geometric patterns, arabesques, or floral motifs. The frames can be made of wood, metal, or a combination of both, often finished with a distressed look to add character and charm.

Hang a large Moroccan mirror in your hallway or living room to create a sense of space and light. Smaller mirrors can be grouped together to form a striking wall display. The reflective surfaces help to brighten the room and highlight the beauty of your decor.

8. Luxurious Textiles and Fabrics

Textiles play a crucial role in Moroccan decor, adding layers of texture and color to your home. From curtains and cushions to throws and bedspreads, luxurious fabrics in rich hues and intricate patterns are essential for creating a cozy and inviting space.

Opt for fabrics in deep reds, blues, and purples, often adorned with gold or silver embroidery. These textiles can feature traditional Moroccan patterns such as geometric shapes, arabesques, and floral designs. Use them to dress your windows, upholster your furniture, or as decorative accents throughout your home.

Layering different textiles is key to achieving the Moroccan look. Mix and match patterns and textures to create a rich, tactile environment. The combination of soft, plush fabrics and intricate designs adds warmth and comfort to any room.

9. Exotic Patterns and Wallpapers

Incorporating exotic patterns and wallpapers is an effective way to bring Moroccan flair into your home. These patterns often feature bold, geometric designs, intricate florals, and vibrant colors. Use them to create an accent wall or to add interest to smaller spaces like alcoves or nooks.

For a dramatic effect, choose wallpapers with traditional Moroccan motifs in rich, jewel tones. These designs can range from intricate zellige patterns to larger, more abstract geometric shapes. The bold colors and detailed patterns create a striking visual impact, transforming even the simplest of spaces.

If you prefer a subtler approach, opt for wallpaper with a more muted color palette or simpler patterns. This allows you to introduce Moroccan elements without overwhelming the room. Pair the wallpaper with complementary decor items, such as Moroccan lanterns, mirrors, and textiles, to complete the look.

10. Low Seating Arrangements

Low seating arrangements are a hallmark of Moroccan interior design, promoting a relaxed and intimate atmosphere. Incorporate low sofas, floor cushions, and poufs to create a cozy seating area that invites conversation and comfort.

Choose seating options upholstered in rich fabrics with intricate patterns and textures. Deep, jewel-toned colors and luxurious materials such as velvet or silk add a touch of elegance and warmth. Arrange the seating around a central coffee table, preferably one with a mosaic or carved wood design.

Enhance the low seating area with additional Moroccan decor elements, such as rugs, lanterns, and textiles. This creates a layered and inviting space perfect for relaxing and entertaining. The combination of comfort and style makes this seating arrangement a standout feature in any room.

11. Moroccan Arches and Doorways

Moroccan architecture is renowned for its distinctive arches and doorways, which add an element of grandeur and elegance to any space. Incorporate these architectural features into your home to capture the essence of Moroccan design.

Consider installing arched doorways or windows to create a seamless flow between rooms. These arches can be simple and understated or elaborately decorated with carved wood or plaster. The curves and shapes of the arches soften the lines of the room and add visual interest.

For an even more authentic touch, look for antique Moroccan doors to use as entryways or as decorative wall pieces. These doors often feature intricate carvings and metalwork, showcasing traditional Moroccan craftsmanship. The blend of artistry and functionality makes them a beautiful addition to your home.

12. Ceramic and Pottery Accents

Ceramic and pottery accents are essential in Moroccan decor, adding both color and texture to your home. These items can be both functional and decorative, featuring traditional Moroccan patterns and designs.

Look for ceramic pieces such as vases, bowls, and platters in vibrant colors and intricate patterns. These can be used to display flowers, hold fruit, or simply as decorative accents on shelves and tables. Moroccan pottery often features bold geometric shapes and detailed motifs that add a touch of artistry to your space.

Incorporate these ceramics into your kitchen, dining room, or living room to create a cohesive Moroccan theme. Mix and match different pieces to create a visually appealing display that captures the essence of Moroccan craftsmanship and style.

13. Vibrant Cushion and Pillow Arrangements

Cushions and pillows are a simple yet effective way to infuse Moroccan style into your home. Opt for vibrant, richly colored fabrics with intricate patterns and embroidery to add a touch of luxury and comfort to your seating areas.

Choose cushions in deep reds, blues, purples, and golds, featuring traditional Moroccan motifs such as geometric shapes, arabesques, and floral designs. Layer these cushions on sofas, chairs, and beds to create a cozy, inviting atmosphere. The mix of colors and patterns adds depth and visual interest to your space.

Don’t be afraid to mix and match different sizes and shapes of cushions. This creates a dynamic and eclectic look that is quintessentially Moroccan. Pair the cushions with complementary textiles, such as throws and rugs, to complete the look.

14. Moroccan Screens and Room Dividers

Screens and room dividers are practical and decorative elements in Moroccan decor, adding an element of privacy and visual interest to your space. These pieces often feature intricate carvings or latticework, showcasing traditional Moroccan craftsmanship.

Use screens to divide larger rooms or to create cozy nooks and alcoves. They can be made from wood, metal, or a combination of materials, often finished with detailed patterns and motifs. The intricate designs of these screens add a touch of elegance and artistry to your home.

Place a screen behind a sofa, bed, or in a corner to create a focal point. Pair it with complementary decor items, such as lanterns, cushions, and rugs, to enhance the overall Moroccan theme. The combination of functionality and beauty makes these screens a valuable addition to any room.
